Identity and Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I was in my mid late teens I had to get my car repaired and was living pay check to pay check so I went to my grandmother for help. We are not a wealthy family so the money I was asking for was significant ($500) and the deal was I would pay it back at $50 a week over two and a half months which was fine with me. Anyway she ended up putting the $500 through twice which may have gone unnoticed on her part as she isn't savvy with that sort of stuff. I considered hanging on to it but returned it because I valued the relationship with my Grandmother and did not want to break that trust. The values involved were honesty and trust I suppose. Looking back, although that money was much needed by me at the time, I made the right decision.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Ive never thought of myself as having a culture growing up, coming from a small family I always liked thinking I was carving my own path and not influenced by those around me. Looking back I know now I was wrong. I grew up around fishing and diving which made me appreciate time spent outdoors, I also think it instilled in me a patience that I may not have gained otherwise. My father taught me how treat people, not by telling me how it should be, just by being who he was.

I did two online tests to have a look what would come from them and I wasnt too surprised. I think my biggest strenght in the context of being a complete bigenner are my patience and my ability to solve problems.

Im not too sure how to answer this. I have plenty of limitations but the honest answer is I don't know yet. Discipline to self learn during and after this course will be my biggest hurdle I feel. Also accepting that things will not be perfect and having the ability to not sit on one thing for too long trying to get it exactly right (like I did when I started making this.)

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

When I was eighteen I was in this army bootcamp. A portion of it was spent camping in the bush and on this particular day we were doing a cross country orientation challenge where we were split into teams. There were no designated leaders but for whatever reason I ended up being the one making the calls. There was some bad blood on my team between myself and this guy which had carried over from other competitions we had been part of together and he was being difficult. As we walked to our next objective I lagged behind the group with him and instead of being confrontational I framed the conversation about being that I needed his help as he was the best at this (which was manipulative) it worked and the rest of the course went well. We ended up placing second. In hind site I may have approached it differently instead of stroking his ego, I'm not sure, it seemed the easiest and quickest resolution at the time and when we started working together we started flying through, he had some good contributions.
