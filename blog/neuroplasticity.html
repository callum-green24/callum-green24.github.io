Neuroplasticity and a Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity... What is it?

It’s the brains ability to change and build new connections through practice and new experiences. Like engaging a muscle group that otherwise lays reasonably dormant and them growing over time.

Something interesting I found while researching this was was Neurogenisis which is the creation of new brain cells throughout adult life, which is not todays topic but still pretty cool.

Benefits of understanding Neuroplasticity

Learning and confidence in learning.
Understanding the benefits of the principles of Neuroplasticity I’ve come to quickly believe is essential especially from a young age. It lets you know that you are not at your peak of intelligence or learning and it is a journey. It lets people understand that repetition and practice can lead to improved skill on whatever they are working on as the brain will rewire.
Another benefit of understanding this ability to rewire or form new connections is in the rehabilitation of people with brain injury.

How can I engage with Neuroplasticity

Learning learning learning. Stay curious and excited about new ideas and delve into new subjects on more than a surface level. Practice.

Mindfulness and Meditation have been shown to be good for Neuroplasticity. Reducing stress, enhancing cognition, changes is brain connections, enhanced neural communication with the networks involves in attention, emotion processing and cognitive control. Regular meditation has also been shown to lead to increased grey matter density in some regions. Meditation has never been something I have had any success in but after learning this I am excited to give it another try.

Ways to increase Neuroplasticity

Treat your body well and form connections with other people. Exercise, eat the right things (Omega-3 Fatty Acids, Antioxidants, B vitamins) and have meaningful relationships.

Growth Mindset

What is it and why is it relevant?

Its the belief that you are not static you have the ability to develop throng hard work and its relevant because it nurtures persistence and not just the willingness to embrace challenges but the understanding that should you fail its just for now as you can continue to grow.

After learning about this was there anything that surprised or changed for me?

Surprised. Sure. I had always thought I had a growth mindset although I had never heard the term or read anything about it but its principles I thought I carried, but thinking back to when I was at school and throughout my life after I have had a very fixed mindset way of thinking. Is it possible to carry both sets of characteristics at the same time? I feel I do.

How will I incorporate a growth mindset into my learning journey?

By taking each challenge and failure as a step forward rather than back, part of my foundation that I can build on. By remaining focused on continued learning.

Reflection on how incorporating these understandings will be beneficial on my coding journey

By incorporating a growth mindset and the understanding of Neuroplasticity I feel I will become a more resilient and skilled coder who can handle setbacks and challenges and be determined to keep moving forward as its all part of growing.
